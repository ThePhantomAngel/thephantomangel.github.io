Every Phantom needs its angel


You’ve just forked out over $100 for a brand new Phantom 2 battery. A few flights later it last less and less time and a few flights after that it puffs up. Game over – time to dispose it properly and fork out another $100.

Like all LiPos (lithium polymer batteries), Phantom batteries need proper care, otherwise they very quickly get permanently and dangerously damaged.

Two simple rules to keep your investment in new batteries to a minimum:
  • Never fly them below 25% capacity
  • Never store them full for more than 24hrs.

These two rules can make the difference between a dozen cycles and hundreds if not thousands.

Up to now there have only been three bad options for discharging your Phantom batteries:
  • Leave them on for days on end (and risk over-discharging them)
  • Discharge them in your Phantom wearing out your electronics and motors
  • If you have the "new" batteries (at least firmware 2.6.0.0) then you can wait for 10 days and they'll self-discharge. This averts total disaster but it still keeps your precious battery at full charge for 10 whole days - way more the maximum storage time allowed to avoid damage

Phantom Angel to the rescue
